## 🎯 Goal
Use a small surrogate model over the `[CLASSIFY]` / `[TEXT_UTILS]` config constants to find which actually move line categorization — **without** rerunning the GPU/FastText/XML pipeline. Ground rules from the opening comment: the input per-line CSVs are the immutable "holy grail" (ground truth never edited), recategorized output goes to a separate dir; preserve current categories, train a surrogate to predict them, and vary the constants across trials. `tools/recategorize_from_csv.py` is the reference entry point for CSV read / recategorize / save.

## 🛠️ Tooling built
- `recategorize_from_csv.py` — replays scoring/categorization from existing CSV features (immutable in, recategorized out); reports flip-rate, macro/weighted F1, category rates, KL shift, confusion matrix.
- `const_importance_sweep.py` — samples config constants, fits a sklearn RandomForest surrogate (optional Optuna/fANOVA), emits parameter-importance rankings.
- `run_ablation_study.py`, `greedy_backward_elimination.py`, `ab_constant_eval.py` — test which rules/constants can be removed or changed.

Expensive signals (`perplex`, `lang_score`, `garbage_density`, `word_weird`, `rot_ratio`, …) are pre-computed in the CSVs, so parametric replay is cheap and reproducible.

## 🏆 Headline finding — the "Big Five"
A handful of Clear↔Noisy boundary constants dominate `macro_f1` variance; the long tail sits at the noise floor. Latest 1000-trial sklearn sweep after refactor `b4bd545` (r²_train ≈ 0.968, OOB r² ≈ 0.809, 36 params):

| Constant | Permutation importance | Role |
|---|---:|---|
| `MOSTLY_READABLE_VALID_MIN` | ~0.528 | dominant Clear/Noisy readability gate |
| `LOWPPL_CLEAR_MAX` | ~0.238 | low-perplexity Clear rescue |
| `CATEG_GARBAGE_DENSITY_HIGH` | ~0.128 | garbage-density cutoff |
| `LOWPPL_CZECH_CLEAR_MAX` | ~0.051 | Czech-specific low-PPL rescue |
| `CATEG_TRASH_SCORE_MAX` | ~0.0067 | much lower now |
| page-context constants | ~0.001–0.002 | low impact in this corpus |

Four constants do ~84% of the work; dozens score <1%. The pre-refactor 33-param / 2000-trial sweep agreed (MDI: `MOSTLY_READABLE_VALID_MIN` 0.296, `LOWPPL_CLEAR_MAX` 0.131, `CATEG_GARBAGE_DENSITY_HIGH` 0.072, `LOWPPL_CZECH_CLEAR_MAX` 0.049). `CLEAR_BAND_WC_MIN` used to rank high; after its removal its weight shifted into `MOSTLY_READABLE_VALID_MIN` / `LOWPPL_CLEAR_MAX`.

## ⚠️ Load-bearing caveat
The CSV `categ` values are produced by the current config, so **baseline macro-F1 = 1.0 and flip-rate = 0 by construction**. The sweep correctly answers *"which constants **can** change the current categorization"* — it does **not** prove *"which are objectively **better** for OCR quality."* That needs gold labels / manual review of flipped lines.

## Part A — My conclusions (the final bottom line)

**1. The goal is achieved as a _sensitivity map_, not as a _recalibration_ — and that
distinction is the whole story.** Issue #5 asked "which constants matter while preserving the
categories." That question is answered well. But because the per-line `categ` labels are
**generated by the current config**, every objective (`macro_f1`, flip_rate, KL) is maximized
at the current config *by construction*. So the machinery can rank *what moves the labels*; it
cannot, on its own, find a config that is *actually better*. Every numeric recommendation is
provisional until config-independent gold labels exist. This is now the consensus across Opus
4.8, GPT-5, and GPT-5.5, and I concur — it is the single most important caveat.

**2. The "delete all 15 rules / Minimal Rule Set: 0" result was a genuine methodological trap,
correctly caught and fixed.** The original cost `min(clear_drop, trash_rise)` only fires when
Clear shrinks **and** Trash grows at once; the dropped rules instead cause *one-directional*
leakage (Trash→Noisy/Clear, Noisy→Clear), so every rule scored cost 0 and the greedy halt
never fired — even while the last dropped rule flipped 306 lines (14% of the corpus). The fix
(use the per-line confusion + macro-F1 that `evaluate_dataframe` already computed and threw
away) is the right one. Lesson worth keeping: **a net-distributional proxy is the wrong cost
function for a ground-truth-preserving study; use per-line agreement.**

**3. The corrected pruning result is believable but corpus-specific.** On the 19-doc /
2,171-line `DOC_LINE_CATEG` corpus: **3 rules** strictly redundant (zero flips, zero F1 cost —
`rule_garbage_density`, `rule_inverted`, `rule_allcaps`), **8** prunable within a 0.2% F1
tolerance, **6 load-bearing** (`penalty_ledger_fragmentation`, `penalty_wqx_rot`,
`rule_extreme_ppl`, `rule_lowppl_clear`, `rule_mostly_readable_noisy`,
`rule_trailing_fill_rescue`). My read: these are **"corpus-redundant," not "dead code."** The
greedy log itself shows rules flipping 0 lines alone but 2 once others are gone (survivor
bias / entanglement). The cheap structural guards (`inverted`, `allcaps`, `garbage_density`)
should stay until multi-collection coverage evidence exists — they cost ~nothing and guard
failure modes absent from this sample.

**4. The importance picture is the robust, defensible finding.** Consistent across the
1000- and 2000-trial sweeps and all three reviewers: a **"Big Four"** does ~84% of the work —
`MOSTLY_READABLE_VALID_MIN` (~0.46), `LOWPPL_CLEAR_MAX` (~0.21),
`CATEG_GARBAGE_DENSITY_HIGH` (~0.12), `LOWPPL_CZECH_CLEAR_MAX` (~0.06) — the 9 `QS_WEIGHT_*`
are **non-identifiable** from the labels (freeze them, don't delete the score), and ~30
constants sit at the ~0.001–0.008 noise floor. The engine is genuinely over-parameterized;
that claim is earned.

**5. The one real tuning lever is confounded and not production-ready.**
`CATEG_GARBAGE_DENSITY_HIGH` 0.35→0.55 is the highest-importance actionable move, but the
constant is reused in **three** places (the hard `rule_garbage_density` gate **and** the QS
garbage normalization **and** the short-line penalty), so its "importance" conflates all three
and moving it loosens all three at once. The A/B claim that 0.55 is *"entirely safe to apply to
production"* (comment, 2026-06-25 05:49) is **overstated** — the A/B only shows a small
*deviation from self-generated labels*, not correctness. GPT-5.5's correction is right: it is a
*candidate worth manual review of the ~4 flipped lines*, nothing more.

**6. The system is already near the surrogate's optimum, so blind retuning has little upside.**
Best-trial flip_rate is ~0.017–0.025 — the current config is within ~2% of the optimum *on this
corpus* with KL ≈ 0 (it isn't gaming F1 by collapsing classes). The leverage now is in
**labeling and decoupling**, not in more sweeping or in copying `best_config` (whose
low-importance params are noise, not recommendations).

**7. The durable deliverable is the tooling, not the numbers.** The immutable-CSV replay, the
`evaluate_dataframe` metric suite, the corrected greedy frontier, and the A/B helper are
genuinely valuable, reusable, laptop-runnable infrastructure. The refactor (removing provably
dead constants/rules + parameterizing the page-context smoothers) is safe and should ship.

> **Bottom line.** Issue #5 set out to "use a small surrogate to find which constants matter
> while preserving categories" — and on that narrow goal it **succeeded**: it produced a
> trustworthy sensitivity map (a few readability/low-PPL/garbage gates run the engine, the
> QS-weight blend is redundant, the long tail is inert) and good offline tooling, and it
> cleaned up provably dead code. What it has **not** done — and cannot, by construction — is
> *recalibrate* the pipeline: the labels are self-generated, so no sweep here can prove a config
> is *better*, only *different*. The responsible close-out is therefore: **ship the tooling +
> the dead-code refactor, freeze the defaults, keep the rules**, and treat the garbage-gate move
> as a reviewed candidate. The next phase is no longer "tune harder" — it is "break the
> self-reference with a small gold set, then decouple, then retune."

## Part B — Potential expansion & improvement

Ordered by leverage. The first three are already proposed in the thread (designed, not yet
implemented — none of `build_label_queue.py`, a Sobol backend, or `QS_GARBAGE_NORM_MAX` exist
in the `test` tree); 4–6 are my additions.

**B1. Break the self-reference with a small gold set (highest value).** Add
`tools/build_label_queue.py` as an **active-learning extractor** that emits a focused
human-labeling queue from exactly the lines that carry the most information: the A/B **flip
set**, **decision-margin** lines (near `CATEG_*_SCORE_MAX` band edges), **rule-boundary** lines
(those a rule fired on), and **high-disagreement** lines across sweep trials. Emit `n_flips`,
`alt_categories`, `margin`, and an empty `gold_categ` column. Then add an optional
`gold_column` path to `evaluate_dataframe` that **scores only the labeled rows against the human
label** (not the self-generated `categ`). This is what converts the whole effort from
"what changes the labels?" into "what is actually better?" — and only then do the A/B and the
sweep become real optimization.

**B2. Decouple `CATEG_GARBAGE_DENSITY_HIGH` (cheap, parity-preserving, unblocks B1's A/B).**
Split the overloaded constant: keep `CATEG_GARBAGE_DENSITY_HIGH` for the **hard
`rule_garbage_density` gate** only, and introduce a new `QS_GARBAGE_NORM_MAX` for the
**quality-score garbage scale** at the three QS sites (`text_util_langID.py` ~L1191, ~L1194,
~L1227). Both default to `0.35` ⇒ **bit-for-bit identical behavior** (parity test stays green),
but the gate and the score can now be A/B'd independently — removing the confound that makes
the 0.55 result un-trustworthy today. Also register `QS_GARBAGE_NORM_MAX` in
`recategorize_from_csv.py` (`_THRESHOLD_NAMES`) and `const_importance_sweep.py`
(`SEARCH_SPACE` + `THRESHOLD_PARAMS`) so it auto-joins the sweep as a 37th tunable.

**B3. Add a model-free, variance-based sensitivity backend (Sobol/Morris).** Add
`--backend morris|sobol` to `const_importance_sweep.py` (via SALib): a cheap **Morris** screen
(`r·(D+1)` evals, ranks by `mu_star`, flags interactions) then **Sobol** (`N·(2D+2)` evals) on
the constraint-free survivors for first-order `S1` vs total-order `ST` indices. This removes the
known RF-MDI bias toward continuous/high-cardinality params and **exposes the coupling** (e.g.
`CATEG_GARBAGE_DENSITY_HIGH`'s three-way reuse). Blocking constraint: Saltelli sampling is a
structured `(A, B, ABᵢ)` matrix, so v1 restricts Sobol to the constraint-free constants. Honest
limit (must keep stating): Sobol still scores against self-generated labels — it is a better
*importance/coupling* tool, **not** a correctness tool.

**B4. Always report per-document, and require cross-backend agreement.** With only 19 documents
and page-level post-processing, a few documents can dominate any global importance number.
Emit `baseline_per_document.json` and treat a ranking as final only when permutation, MDI, and
Sobol broadly agree. Run the real studies on the **full corpus on the cluster** — the bundled
`DOC_LINE_CATEG` is explicitly a smoke fixture (`tools/SWEEP_NOTES.md`), not a tuning basis.

**B5. Coverage-gated rule retirement.** Before deleting any of the 8 within-tolerance rules,
verify on **multiple collections** that the rule never fires (or fires only on lines it agrees
with). Retire only rules that are *both* corpus-redundant *and* coverage-empty across
collections; keep the cheap structural guards regardless.

**B6. (Longer term) Replace the threshold stack with a small interpretable model.** Once a gold
set exists (B1), consider swapping the hand-tuned constant cascade for a **monotonic,
interpretable** classifier (monotonic GBM / calibrated logistic regression) over the same
pre-computed features. Same transparency and auditability, but it ends the "threshold
treadmill" and learns the boundaries from human truth instead of from itself. Optionally make
the objective **cost-sensitive** (asymmetric: a Clear→Trash loss is worse than a Trash→Noisy
leak), which directly encodes the downstream rationale the Gemini report highlighted —
aggressive upstream filtering exists to protect the scarce VRAM of the 27B/31B enrichment LLMs.
