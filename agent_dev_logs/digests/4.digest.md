## 🎯 Goal & Core Constraint
Fully document the ALTO text quality categorization algorithm, detailing the shift to strict quality-score thresholds, edge cases, and post-processing overrides. Establish a feedback loop with human annotators to refine rule definitions based on real-world misclassifications.

## 🏆 Headline Findings & Conclusions

**1. Categorization Logic Overhauled and Documented**
The algorithm's core logic was modified, replacing the legacy cumulative penalty system with strict quality-score thresholds. The `README.md` has been extensively expanded to document the immediate overrides, the Mostly-Readable Cap, and the post-processing smoothing rules.

**2. Czech Prose and Measurement Protections**
Several structural invariants were refined to protect valid domain-specific data:
* A `rot_ratio` and mirroring logic adjustment was implemented to ensure legitimate Czech text is not misclassified as inverted garbage by the page-level and per-line sweeps.
* The `symbol` detector whitelist was corrected to include specific punctuation (such as dashes and colons) that are common in historical measurement notation, preventing false `Non-text` or `Trash` classifications.

**3. Human Annotation Feedback Loop**
A structured process was established for end-user testing, and a **full cycle has now completed**: Dana Křivánková's 2026-07-02 review (tracked in [#3](https://github.com/ufal/atrium-alto-postprocess/issues/3)) delivered five concrete findings (short abbreviations, numbered headlines, all-caps headlines, the 0.85→0.80 Noisy↔Clear boundary, unconditional lang-score remap); the resulting rule edits shipped in **v0.20.1** (2026-07-03), and the `README.md` documents the post-pass logic (0.80 boundary, forgiven-headline rescue, `LANG_REMAP_ALWAYS` switch).

## 🏁 Final Bottom Line
The primary documentation effort in the markdown files is complete and **current with the shipped v0.20.1 logic**.
