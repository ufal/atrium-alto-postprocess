## 🎯 Goal
Define the five OCR-quality categories and the logic that assigns them. Opening prompts:
regex for digits-attached-to-letters and symbols-inside-words as a *separate measure*, and
the option to **ignore a poorly-scored language** during assignment.

## 🏷️ The five categories (recoverability is the axis)
The core insight from the expert review (motyc, with Dana & Tomáš): the distinction between
categories is **recoverability of the original text**.
- **Clear** ✅ — linguistically correct, immediately usable.
- **Noisy** ⚠️ — mostly readable, OCR artefacts; meaning still recoverable.
- **Trash** 🗑️ — heavily corrupted; original meaning not confidently reconstructable.
- **Non-text** 🔣 — structured but not language (numbers, codes, measurements).
- **Empty** 🫙 — no usable content.

Strong inter-annotator agreement on **Clear / Non-text / Empty**; disagreement concentrates on
**Noisy↔Clear**, **Noisy↔Trash**, **Non-text↔Trash** — which is exactly where the later #3
calibration spends its effort.

## 🧾 Expert-review input (motyc, 2026-03-19)
`Vse_260318.csv` carried Dana + Tomáš (DK/TK) expert category columns on a real sample, plus a
ChatGPT pattern analysis offered as *non-canonical but worth trying*. Its recommendations
seeded the implementation: symbol-ratio as the most important feature, valid-word-ratio,
digit-ratio + regex for Non-text, perplexity as a **supporting signal only**, and — crucially
— a **scoring approach preferred over strict rules** (`score = 0.4·valid + 0.3·(1−symbol) +
0.2·perplexity + 0.1·length`). That preference is what the codebase ultimately adopted.

## 📊 Column contract (as documented for DanaKriv, 2026-04-17)
Input columns: `file`, `page_num`, `line_num`, `text`, `split_ws`/`split_we` (hyphenation),
`lang`, `lang_score` (FastText), `perplex` (DistilGPT2). Added measures: `word_count`,
`char_count`, `garbage_density`, `symbol`, `upper`, `repeated`, `ldl_fuses`, `gibberish`,
`word_weird` (mean per-word weirdness ∈ [0,1]). Decision column: **`quality_score`** ∈ [0,1]
(valid-word ratio + symbol ratio + perplexity + length). Output column: `categ`.

### Structural detectors (`text_util_langID.py`)
`detect_strange_symbols` (disallowed internal chars, allowed set `{ . - , + ( ) " ' / _ — – :
% }`), `detect_letter_digit_letter` (LDL sandwich `vyt1ačená`; legitimate `90,9g`, `26.IX.1957`
excluded), `detect_mid_uppercase` (`dalSÍ`; titles `PhDr`/`MUDr` excluded), `detect_repeated_chars`
(≥40% single non-standard char), `detect_gibberish_words` (len ≥7 all-caps / no-vowel / extreme
vowel ratio).

### CPU pre-filter (before any GPU/model)
Blank → **Empty**; <4 chars or <3 unique symbols → **Non-text**; letter ratio <30% → **Non-text**;
all-digits/symbols regex → **Non-text**; else → forwarded to ML as **Process**.

## 🔁 Scoring-model evolution (important: the thread shows a *superseded* system)
The 2026-04-17 thread documents a **cumulative-penalty** categoriser (immediate Trash overrides
on garbage-density / `ppl>500 ∧ weird>0.4`; additive per-signal penalties; normalised-penalty
thresholds `≥1.2 → Trash`, `≥0.3 → Noisy`). **This is not the current implementation.** On
`test` HEAD the cumulative penalty was replaced by a pure **quality-score** decision —
`quality_score` *is* the categorisation factor, with `CATEG_TRASH_SCORE_MAX = 0.55` and
`CATEG_NOISY_SCORE_MAX = 0.80` as the Trash/Noisy/Clear boundaries (see #3 commit `646fc5e`:
"`quality_score` becomes the sole categorization factor"; the Noisy↔Clear boundary was
lowered 0.85 → **0.80** by the #3 2026-07-02 calibration pass, shipped in **v0.20.1** —
`config_langID.txt:61`). The penalty mechanics survive only as **score adjustments** inside
`compute_quality_score`, not as the final decision.

> Perplexity is deliberately **not** a standalone Trash signal — `distilgpt2` is English and
> over-penalises legitimate short Czech strings (place names, postal codes, form labels). It
> contributes only as a context-gated component of the score. (This guard persists; the hub
> review keeps the `PERPLEXITY_THRESHOLD_MAX` Qwen guard as advisory, not a bug.)

## 🪄 Post-processing smoothing (still present)
1. **Header/footer dedup** — identical text strings across a document harmonise to their modal
   category. 2. **3-line rolling window** — a Noisy line between two Trash lines downgrades to
   Trash. (These became the parameterised page-context smoothing thresholds analysed in #5/B5.)
